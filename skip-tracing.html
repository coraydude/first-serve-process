From bad address to completed serve

A lawsuit can't move until the defendant is served — and a defendant can't be served at an address they left two years ago. Skip tracing bridges that gap: locating current residences, workplaces, and vehicles using licensed databases, public records, and field verification.

Our tracers cross-reference utility connects, credit headers, vehicle registrations, and county records, then verify the strongest candidate address before recommending service. You get a documented locate report either way — useful if you later need to ask the court for alternative service.

Because we're also process servers, there's no handoff delay: the same order flows from locate straight to attempt.

Skip tracing questions

How long does skip tracing take?

Most Florida locates complete within 24–72 hours. Complex cases (common names, recent moves, intentional concealment) can take longer — we'll set expectations up front.

What information do you need to start?

Full name plus any of: last known address, date of birth, phone number, employer, or vehicle. The more identifiers, the faster and more certain the locate.

Is skip tracing legal?

Yes, when done for permissible purposes like service of process, using lawful data sources. Our tracing is compliant with the FCRA, GLBA, and DPPA.

What if you can't find them?

You receive a documented no-locate report detailing everything checked — which supports a motion for alternative service (such as service by publication) if your case needs it.
