From bad address to completed serve in Tampa, Miami & Orlando

A lawsuit can't move until the defendant is served — and a defendant can't be served at an address they left years ago. Skip tracing bridges that gap: locating current residences, workplaces, and vehicles using licensed databases, public records, and on-the-ground field verification anywhere in Florida.

Our Florida skip tracers cross-reference utility connects, credit headers, vehicle registrations, and county property records, then verify the strongest candidate address before recommending service. Either way, you receive a documented locate report — the kind of record that supports a motion for alternative service if the court later requires it.

Because we're also full-service Florida process servers, there's no handoff delay: the same order flows straight from locate to serve attempt, from the Panhandle to the Keys.

Florida skip tracing questions

How long does skip tracing take in Florida?

Most Florida locates complete within 24–72 hours. Complex cases — common names, recent moves, or intentional concealment — can take longer, and we'll set clear expectations up front so your deadline isn't a surprise.

What information do you need to start a skip trace?

A full name plus any of the following: last known address, date of birth, phone number, employer, or vehicle. The more identifiers you provide, the faster and more certain the locate.

Is skip tracing legal?

Yes — when done for a permissible purpose such as service of process, using lawful data sources. Our tracing is compliant with the FCRA, GLBA, and DPPA, with no pretexting.

What happens if you can't find the person?

You receive a documented no-locate report detailing everything checked. That record supports a motion for alternative service — such as service by publication or substitute service — if your Florida case needs it.

Do you cover skip tracing in every Florida county?

Yes. We locate and serve subjects across all 67 Florida counties, from Tampa Bay and Central Florida to Miami, Fort Lauderdale, Jacksonville, and the Panhandle, with nationwide reach through our partner network.

Can you skip trace an evasive defendant who is actively avoiding service?

Yes. We combine database tracing with field verification, and when a defendant is confirmed at an address but dodging the door, we can escalate to stakeout services so the serve gets completed.
