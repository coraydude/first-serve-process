Florida document retrieval questions

How fast can you retrieve a Florida court record?

Records available at the counter are usually retrieved within a couple of business days. Archived or off-site files can take longer; we confirm timing with the clerk before you commit, so there are no surprises.

What's the difference between certified and exemplified copies?

A certified copy bears the clerk's seal attesting it's a true copy. An exemplified (triple-seal) copy adds judge and clerk attestations — required for domesticating a Florida judgment in another state.

Can you retrieve sealed or confidential records?

Only with proper authorization — a court order or verified standing as a party or counsel. We'll tell you exactly what authorization the clerk requires before we attempt retrieval.

Do you retrieve non-court records too?

Yes — official records like deeds, liens, and mortgages from any Florida county recorder, plus corporate records from the Division of Corporations and vital records where lawfully available.

Which Florida counties do you retrieve records from?

All 67 Florida counties. We pull records from courthouses statewide, including Hillsborough (Tampa), Orange (Orlando), Miami-Dade, Duval (Jacksonville), Pinellas (St. Petersburg), and Broward (Fort Lauderdale).

Can you retrieve records if I only have partial case information?

Often, yes. If you can share a party name, approximate date, or the county involved, our team can search the clerk's index and locate the file — including older cases that were re-indexed or moved to an archive.
