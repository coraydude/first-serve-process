Document retrieval questions

How fast can you retrieve a court record?

Records available at the counter are usually retrieved within 1–3 business days. Archived or off-site files can take longer; we confirm timing with the clerk before you commit.

What’s the difference between certified and exemplified copies?

A certified copy bears the clerk’s seal attesting it’s a true copy. An exemplified (triple-seal) copy adds judge and clerk attestations — required for domesticating judgments in other states.

Can you retrieve sealed or confidential records?

Only with proper authorization — a court order or verified standing as a party or counsel. We’ll tell you what authorization the clerk requires before we attempt retrieval.

Do you retrieve non-court records too?

Yes — official records like deeds, liens, and mortgages, plus corporate records from the Division of Corporations and vital records where lawfully available.
