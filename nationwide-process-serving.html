Nationwide process serving questions

How long does out-of-state service of process take?

A first attempt typically takes several business days depending on the state and locality, with rush options available in most major metros. We confirm expected timing when we quote your job.

Whose rules apply to an out-of-state serve?

Generally the destination state’s rules govern how service is performed, while your issuing court’s rules govern the proof required. We manage both sides so the affidavit and return of service work everywhere they need to.

Can you serve a registered agent in another state?

Yes. Corporate and registered-agent service is among our most common nationwide work, including bulk campaigns across multiple states from a single Florida point of contact.

Do you handle international service of process?

We coordinate service abroad under the Hague Service Convention and other applicable channels. International timelines run months rather than days, so contact us early in your case.

Can I combine Florida and out-of-state serves on one job?

Yes. Many cases involve defendants in Tampa, Miami, or Orlando alongside witnesses out of state. We coordinate your Florida serves and nationwide serves together, with one dispatcher and one consolidated invoice.

What if the out-of-state defendant is evasive?

Evasive defendants are handled by experienced local servers who document each attempt and, where permitted, pursue substitute service under the destination state’s rules. If an address goes cold, our skip tracing team can locate a current one before we serve.
