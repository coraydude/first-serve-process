Florida mobile notary questions

How quickly can a mobile notary reach me?

Same-day appointments are usually available in major Florida metros like Orlando, Tampa, Miami, and Jacksonville; most appointments are scheduled within 24 hours statewide.

What ID does the signer need?

A current government-issued photo ID (driver license, passport, or state ID). Florida also permits credible witness identification in limited cases — ask us if ID is an issue.

Can you notarize for someone in a hospital or facility?

Yes — we do bedside and facility signings across Florida regularly. The signer must be alert, aware, and signing willingly; we coordinate timing with family and facility staff.

Do you offer remote online notarization (RON)?

Yes. Florida authorizes RON, and our online notaries can complete many notarizations by secure video with identity proofing — useful for out-of-state signers and clients who can’t meet in person.

Which areas do your mobile notaries cover?

We travel throughout all 67 Florida counties, including Central Florida, Tampa Bay, South Florida, and North Florida. Tell us the signer’s location and we’ll arrange a notary near you.

Can you notarize real estate closing and loan documents?

Yes. Our NNA-certified signing agents handle full loan packages and real estate closings, presenting and notarizing each document with lender-ready execution.
