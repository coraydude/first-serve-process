Rush service questions

How fast is rush process serving?

In most Florida metros, rush orders are attempted within 2–6 hours of receipt during business hours. Statewide, first attempts happen the same or next business day.

How is rush different from same-day service?

Rush prioritizes your serve at the front of the queue with the fastest available attempt. Same-day service guarantees an attempt on the calendar day documents are received (by the 2pm cutoff).

Does rush service cost more?

Yes — rush carries a priority fee on top of standard service. We quote the full amount up front before you commit; request a quote for exact pricing for your county.

What if the rush attempt fails?

We continue attempts at varied times and escalate to skip tracing or stakeout options immediately, keeping you informed so you can make deadline decisions in real time.
