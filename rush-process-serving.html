Rush process serving questions

How fast is rush process serving in Florida?

In most Florida metros — Orlando, Tampa, Miami, Jacksonville, St. Petersburg and Fort Lauderdale — rush orders are attempted within a few hours of receipt during business hours. Statewide, first attempts happen the same or next business day, typically well inside a 24–48 hour window.

How is rush different from same-day service?

Rush prioritizes your serve at the front of the queue with the fastest available attempt. Same-day service guarantees an attempt on the calendar day documents are received when they come in by the afternoon cutoff.

Does rush process serving cost more?

Yes — rush carries a priority fee on top of standard service of process. We quote the full amount up front before you commit, so there are no surprises; request a quote for exact pricing in your Florida county.

What if the rush attempt fails?

We continue attempts at varied times of day and escalate to skip tracing or stakeout options immediately, keeping you informed at every step so you can make deadline decisions in real time.

Which Florida counties do you cover for rush service?

All 67 Florida counties. From the Tampa Bay area and Central Florida to South Florida and the Panhandle, we dispatch the licensed process server nearest your service address to move a rush order fast.

Can you rush service on an evasive defendant?

Yes. For a defendant who is avoiding service, we combine expedited attempts with skip tracing and, where warranted, substitute service consistent with Florida Statutes Chapter 48 — and we document each attempt so the record supports your case.
