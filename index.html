FAQ

Common questions about process serving

How fast can you serve documents in Florida?

Standard service includes a first attempt within 24–48 hours. Rush and same-day process service is available in most Florida metros, with first attempts often made within hours of receiving your documents.

How much does a process server cost in Florida?

Pricing depends on service type, county, and urgency. Standard serves start at a flat rate per address, with transparent add-ons for rush and same-day service. Request a free quote and you'll have exact pricing within 15 minutes during business hours.

What if the person can't be located?

Our licensed skip tracing locates current addresses and workplaces using legal databases. If a subject is evasive, we use varied attempt times, stakeouts, and alternative service methods permitted under Florida law.

Do I get proof of service for court?

Yes. Every completed serve includes a notarized, court-compliant affidavit (proof of service) delivered digitally, with GPS timestamps and photo evidence. Court filing of the affidavit is available on request.

Can you serve evasive defendants?

Absolutely — it's one of our specialties. We combine skip tracing, multiple attempts across days and hours, and stakeout services to serve subjects who are actively avoiding service.
