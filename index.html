FAQ

Common questions about Florida process serving

How fast can you serve documents in Florida?

Standard service of process includes a first attempt within 24–48 hours. Rush and same-day process service is available in Tampa, Miami, Orlando, Jacksonville, and most Florida metros, with first attempts often made within hours of receiving your documents.

How much does a process server cost in Florida?

Pricing depends on service type, county, and urgency. Standard serves start at a flat rate per address, with transparent add-ons for rush and same-day service. Request a free quote and you'll have exact pricing within 15 minutes during business hours.

What if the person can't be located?

Our licensed skip tracing locates current Florida addresses and workplaces using legal databases. If a subject is evasive, we use varied attempt times, stakeouts, and alternative service methods permitted under Florida law.

Do I get proof of service for court?

Yes. Every completed serve includes a notarized, court-compliant affidavit (return of service) delivered digitally, with GPS timestamps and photo evidence — formatted and ready to file with the court.

Can you serve evasive defendants?

Absolutely — it's one of our specialties. We combine skip tracing, multiple attempts across days and hours, and stakeout services to serve subjects who are actively avoiding service anywhere in Florida.

Which Florida counties do you serve?

We serve all 67 Florida counties statewide — including Hillsborough, Miami-Dade, Orange, Duval, Pinellas, Broward, and Palm Beach — with local process servers who know each courthouse and clerk. Out-of-state documents are handled through our nationwide partner network.

Do you handle substitute service under Florida law?

Yes. When personal service isn't possible, our licensed process servers complete substitute service in accordance with Florida Statutes Chapter 48, documenting each attempt so the return of service holds up in court.

Can you serve summons and subpoenas the same day?

In most Florida metros, yes. Send us the summons or subpoena and request rush or same-day process service, and we'll begin first attempts within hours of receiving your documents.
