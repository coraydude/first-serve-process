Serving evasive defendants in Tampa, Miami, Orlando & every Florida county

Some defendants learn the sound of a process server at the door. They stop answering, park around the corner, and let the mail pile up. Routine repeat attempts drain your budget on people like this — a coordinated stakeout does not. Our Florida process servers turn evasion into an evidentiary record instead of a stalled case.

A stakeout begins with intelligence: our skip trace data on vehicles, work schedules, and daily routines. Servers then hold position at the residence or workplace, confirm the subject's presence, and complete service of process at the moment of contact — commonly on arrival or departure.

If a defendant still cannot be served in person, our detailed surveillance logs give your attorney exactly what Florida courts want to see before authorizing substitute or constructive service under Chapter 48. Whether the address sits in Hillsborough, Miami-Dade, Orange, Duval, or Broward County, the diligence record travels with your case.

Florida stakeout & evasive-defendant questions

How long does a typical stakeout last?

Most are 2–4 hour sessions targeted at high-probability windows — early-morning departures and evening arrivals. You set the budget cap, and we recommend windows based on skip trace data for the address.

Is surveillance for service of process legal in Florida?

Yes. Observing from lawful public vantage points to effect service of process is permitted. Our Florida servers follow Chapter 48 and avoid trespass, harassment, and pretext.

What if the defendant never appears?

You receive the full surveillance log regardless. Combined with prior attempt records, it forms the due-diligence evidence Florida courts expect before authorizing substitute or constructive service.

Can you serve someone in their car?

Yes. Florida permits personal service wherever the person is found, including vehicle stops in driveways or parking lots, handled safely and professionally.

Do you run stakeouts across all 67 Florida counties?

We do. Our stakeout teams cover the major metros — Tampa, Miami, Orlando, Jacksonville, St. Petersburg, and Fort Lauderdale — and reach every one of Florida's 67 counties for evasive-defendant service.

How quickly can a stakeout be arranged?

Once we have the address and a skip trace profile, deployment can typically begin within 24–48 hours, and sooner for time-critical injunctions. Tell us the deadline and we build the plan around it.
