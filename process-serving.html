Process serving questions

Who can legally serve process in Florida?

Florida requires service by a sheriff, or by a certified or court-appointed process server per Florida Statutes Chapter 48. All of our servers hold the appointments required in the circuits where they serve.

How many attempts do you make?

Standard service includes multiple attempts at varied times of day, including evenings and weekends, before we recommend next steps like skip tracing or substitute service.

What proof do I receive for court?

A notarized affidavit (return) of service documenting who was served, when, where, and how — with GPS and photo evidence retained on file. We can also file it with the court for you.

What if the person avoids service?

Evasive defendants are our specialty. We combine varied-time attempts, stakeouts, and skip tracing, and we document everything needed if the court permits alternative service.
