Florida process serving questions

Who can legally serve process in Florida?

Florida requires service by a sheriff, or by a certified or court-appointed process server per Florida Statutes Chapter 48. All of our servers hold the appointments required in the circuits where they serve, from Orlando and Tampa to Miami and Jacksonville.

How many attempts do you make?

Standard service includes multiple attempts at varied times of day, including evenings and weekends, before we recommend next steps like skip tracing or substitute service.

What proof do I receive for court?

A notarized affidavit (return) of service documenting who was served, when, where, and how — with GPS and photo evidence retained on file.

What if the person avoids service?

Evasive defendants are our specialty. We combine varied-time attempts, stakeouts, and skip tracing, and we document everything needed if the court permits substitute or alternative service under Chapter 48.

How much does a process server cost in Florida?

Cost depends on the county, the number of documents, and how quickly you need service. Standard, rush, and same-day serves are each priced clearly up front — contact us with your address and deadline and we'll confirm a flat quote before any attempt is made.

Do you serve papers in every Florida county?

Yes. We cover all 67 Florida counties, including the major metros of Orlando, Tampa, Miami, Jacksonville, St. Petersburg, and Fort Lauderdale — and we extend to all 50 states through our nationwide partner network.

How fast can you serve documents?

Standard service typically sees a first attempt within 24–48 hours of receiving your documents, and rush or same-day service is available statewide when a filing deadline is close.
